Abstract-This paper presents a fast-response BUCK converter with adaptive transient detect technique (ATDT) and transient enhancement technique (TET). In order to reduce output overshoot and undershoot voltages and achieve shorter recovery time after load change, an adaptive transient detect module is adopted to determine one-shot transient response time, duty cycle will be set to 100% or 0% instantaneously and regulation speed of compensation capacitor voltage will be also enhanced during this time. Besides, oscillation issue is effectively avoided with proposed TET. The presented fast-response BUCK has been implemented in a 0.35μm BCD process,whose verified results are provided to demonstrate the stability and fast response of proposed converter. Compared with conventional BUCK converter, the transient response time is speeded up by more than 2.4X ,and the overshoot voltage is shrunk by 0.64X while achieving 0.42X undershoot voltage.
Keywords-buck converter; fast transient responese; recovery time; output overshoot/undershoot; active capacitor

I. INTRODUCTION

As areas like computer science, automatic technology and IOT, etc. are rapidly developing, more demands for high quality DC-DC converters with high speed and stability are required. Capability of fast transient response of regulated output voltage is becoming critical, especially in case of some aggressive load change, for example, change from no load to full load or versa.
Various studies have been reported to improve transient response of DC-DC converters. Some new kind of hysteretic control methods are proposed [1], [2], where current information is added to accelerate the transient response and clock signals are injected to reduce EMI noise. However, its control method is quite complicate with lots of additional modules. More researches focus on the improvements of pulse-width-modulation (PWM) control because of its simplicity and widely using. Circuits about slew rate enhancement (SRE) for error amplifier (EA) are added at the cost of local positive feedback, which may result in instable issues [3]. Adaptive phase margin (APM) technique is introduced by [4] to avoid insufficient phase margin or excessive phase margin, which shortens recovery time little, and recovery time is still mainly determined by converter bandwidth without temporary aggressive control.
Despite of SRE technique, current mode capacitor multiplication [5] are also used to realize variable compensation capacitance [6], [7]. The effective compensation capacitance can change tens times to hundred times during transient response time (TRT), which can extend converter bandwidth and speed up transient response. However, in these works, duty cycle still unable to change to extremely high or low due to analog and continuous duty cycle control.
In this work, a fast transient DC-DC buck converter with adaptive transient detect technique (ATDT) and transient enhancement technique (TET) is proposed. Duty cycle will be set to 0% or 100% during TRT to reduce overshoot and undershoot. By this method, the regulation capability of the system is maximized to speed up the control modulation. Besides, regulation speed of loop compensation capacitor voltage will be enhanced at the same time to shorten recovery time. What' more, an anti-oscillation control method is also embedded in the proposed TET to avoid oscillation, which is a common issue with linear and nonlinear hybrid systems. With the help of proposed techniques, the presented BUCK converter can operate at comparatively low frequency with high output ampere level current, while remaining low undershoot and overshoot with short recovery time.
The principle of the proposed control method and system architecture will be introduced in Section II. Critical circuits implemented in proposed converter are described in Section III. Whole chip simulation results with comparison will be shown in Section IV. Finally, conclusion will be made in Section V.

B. Transient Controll Of Convertional And Proposed Method

Since control loop bandwidth is limited to smaller than 1/2 operating frequency with a typical value of 1/4−1/6 operating frequency, the transient response speed has a limitation with operating frequency. Therefore, TET is requited to break the limits. Insufficient TET may result in long recovery time while excessive TET may lead to double shoots or instability. Transient response time should be determined to avoid instable issues.
Constant transient response time (TRT) is adopted in [3] and [6], which is unable to meet the requirements of wide-range load change applications. More researches adopt Threshold Detect method to boost transient response, where TET will be determined by time when feedback (FB) voltage deviates from reference voltage more than a threshold voltage (shown as TRT b and TRT c in Fig.2) [4], [5], [7], however, tradeoff still need to be made.
For example, when load currents rapidly changes from heavy to light, output response of converter with Threshold Detect method is shown in Fig. 2 (b) and (c). The brown line in the picture stands for conservative threshold fast transient control method. Fast transient control starts working after output voltage higher than V tran th ,while conservative control was adopted and duty cycle was changed in a slower manner. The overshoot voltage and recovery time can be smaller than that of conventional one, which is shown in Fig. 2 (a). However, overshoot isn't smallest.
The red line stands for aggressive threshold fast transient control method, where duty cycle can change to minimum until output voltage is lower than V_tran_th. Overshoot is smallest but double shoots may be induced because of excessive regulation.
Different from threshold fast transient control, transient detect module is adopted to generate TRT in proposed design, which was shown as TRT d in Fig. 2. Proposed TRT starts from when load current is suddenly changed to when slew rate of output voltage recover to zero, which means that output current has been regulate to load current. The proposed transient response has been shown in Fig. 2 (d). When load change was detected, Vsum  will be pushed to high or low level depending on the sign of output voltage slew rate. With negative or positive slew rate of output voltage, the duty cycle will be set to 100% or 0% to reduce undershoot or overshoot. What's more, when undershoot is detected, signal VO_L in Fig. 1 will be used to skip clock signal, and HDRV will turn on immediately but no need to wait for clock signal, which can reduce undershoot further.
In order to shorten recovery time, regulation speed of compensation capacitor voltage EA_OUT will be enhanced during generated TRT. A capacitorless active capacitor was adopted to realize a nanofarad equivalent capacitance for converter stability when converter is operating at steady state. Effective capacitor can be reduced to one tenth or smaller than that of steady state during TRT and voltage EA_OUT can be changed faster to accelerate the transient response speed further.

III. Circuits Implimented

A. Transient Detect Module (TDM)

The proposed TDM was designed to measure the slew rate of output voltage, and transient control will start working when slew rate of output voltage is too positive or negative, which stands for overshoot and undershoot respectively. Besides, transient control will quit when slew rate recover to zero. What's more, One Shot and Lock Module was adopted to lock transient response after one response to avoid multi-trigger or even instable issues.
TDM consists of four blocks and its schematic is illustrated in Fig. 3. DC information was filtered by C1 and MR ,where MR is a huge equivalent resistor. Since the DC operation point of this circuit was determined by Ibias  and transistor Mbias  without the effect of stable output voltage,the proposed TDM is suitable for a wide range of output voltage. Voltage ripple and noise over operating frequency are also be filter by RP and CP to protect TDM from spurious triggering. The transfer function from VO to VO_filter is a bandpass filter to select some certain spectrum components in output voltage.

A differentiator formed by Amp2,RD and CD is used to presents the slew rate of output voltage through the voltage on resistance RD . The output of comparator tran H will trigger to high when voltage on resistance RD higher than +Vth_tran  while tran_L will goes high when it lower than −Vth_tran  ,respectively. The sign of output voltage's slew rate can be represented by this method.
The logic schematic of One Shot and Lock Module is shown in Fig. 4 and timing waveform of propose TDM at transient period is shown in Fig. 5. As shown in Fig. 5, proposed TDM can detect and response rapid load change at t1, and signal Delay_rd is reset at this time. But EN/LOCK signal is still unchanged until slew rate of VO changes to zero and VO starts recover at t2, signal TRAN was low for a short period and EN/LOCK went down to lock transient response. As a result, transient response couldn't be triggered when VO is recovering.
Although VO recovered at t3 but signal TRAN was triggered at t4 again, time from t3 to t4 is too short for Delar rd to recover to high. As a result, this rapid change won't be responded and Delay_rd was reset again until VO recover again at t5. It's long enough for Delay_rd to recover to high from t5 to t6 and EN/LOCK signal triggered to high and transient response was enable again at t6. This method can protect system from multi-trigger of fast transient response, which may result to instable issues.

IV. FULL CHIP SIMULATION RESULT

The proposed fast transient DC-DC buck converter with ATDT and TET is simulated in a 0.35μm BCD process. The system specifications are given as follows.
  • Input voltage range Vin =3.3∼6.5V ,output voltage range. Vout =1.0∼3.7V ,nominal switching frequency f=300kHz (at Vin =6.5V and Vout =3.0V ).
  • Power stage capacitor CL=26μF ,inductor L=4.7μH , output current range <6A .
Fig. 7 shows the transient response of proposed DC-DC buck converter with ATDT and TET in a step load change between 1A and 4A (at Vin =6.5V and Vout =3V ). The overshoot is only 314mV and recovery time is 12μs when load current is changed from 4A to 1A ,the TRT is 5.56μs and duty cycle is clamped to zero during this time, which minimizes output overshoot. The undershoot is only 223mV and recovery time is 16μs when load current is changed from 1A to 4A , TRT is 4.74μs and duty cycle is 100 during this time,in addition, compare VO of blue trace to that of green trace, HDRV is turned on right after TRT start, which reduce under shoot more. Comparison of output voltage with proposed techniques (blue trace) to conventional one (red trace) is also shown in Fig. 7, both overshoot, undershoot and recovery time are reduced more than 50%
Transient response of proposed DC-DC buck converter with ATDT and TET in a step load change between 50mA and 2A is also presented in Fig. 8. The adaptive TRT is smaller because of smaller load step. Overshoot and undershoot are reduced more than 50%

The proposed DC-DC buck converter with ATDT and TET is suitable to comparatively low frequency and ampere level output current applications, at the same time, it can strengthen the quality of the power source in power management modules. What's more, proposed technique can still be useful at high switching frequency, ripple of output voltage will be smaller and TRT can be detected more accurately, as a result, transient response can be better as well.

V. Conclusion

This paper presents a fast response DC-DC buck converter with ATDT and TET and achieves excellent transient response. New transient detect method and control method can change duty cycle timely and rapidly, which reduce overshoot and undershoot voltage greatly. Both overshoot, undershoot and settle time are 50% or less than that of without proposed techniques. Conclusion can be made that proposed converter is suitable for applications that require fast transient response over a large load rang without the requirement of high switching frequency, which can release the difficulties of high frequency in DC-DC converters, especially in high voltage applications.
